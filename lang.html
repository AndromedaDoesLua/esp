Espelià Language

Espelià Language:

1. Phonetics and Sounds
Vowels: a, e, i, o, u
Consonants: b, c, d, f, g, h, j, k, l, m, n, p, q, r, s, t, v, w, x, y, z
Accents:
same as in spanish and portuguese pronunciation-wise
    Grave ()
    Tilde (∼)
    Acute (´)

2. Basic Vocabulary

General Words:
Hello: Olàs (neutral)
Goodbye/Bye: Adío (feminine)
Yes: Sí (masculine)
No: Nào (masculine)
Thank: Graćia (feminine)
You: Tù (neutral)
Happen: Tècer (neutral)
Eat: Etàs (masculine)
Drink: Bèber (feminine)
Know: Sàbon (feminine)
Let: Dèt (masculine)
Translate: Tradùciòn (neutral)
Intrigue: Intriguà (masculine)
Please: Fàvor (feminine)
None: Noná (neutral)
All: Todos (neutral)
Less: Menòs (masculine)
More: Más (feminine)

Connectors (always neutral):
in: en
and: ỳ
or: á
for: pòr
at: àt
to: tò
do: dó
on: ón
the: el (masculine word ahead), la (feminine word ahead), le (neutral word ahead)
is: es
it: it
go/start: ir
am: soỳ
how: ćomo
was: fùi
a: ùn
but: pèro
also: tàmbem

Subjects (always neutral):
I / Me: yò
You: tù
My: yes
It: it
Them: elà
They: eles
Company: the company name, nothing special

2.1. Subjects sub-section (always neutral):
Mom = Mamà
Dad = Papà
Person = Persòna

2.2. Starters:
to: áte-
big: ćat-
small: naćat-
ing: inèt-

starters/prefixes copy the gender of the word it is attached to

examples of a starter:

áte-tradùciòn - to translate, translator, the action of translating something

2.3. Nations:
spain: Espàna
portugal: Portùgale
france: Franće
italy: Itàlia
germany: Gèrmanià
UK: UA (placeholder, united is Unidò, but i'm not sure for kingdom)
Switzerland: Switzèterra
Austria: Ostrià
Poland: Polèno
Russia: Rùsia

2.4. Positive words
Good: Bièn (masculine)
Great: ćat-Bièn (masculine)

2.5. Negative words
Bad: Màl (feminine)
Horrible: ćat-Màl (feminine)

2.6. Neutral words
Fine: Màlbièn/Biènmàl
Okay: Ok

3. Grammar and Structure

    Sentence Structure: Subject-Verb-Object (SVO)
    Tenses:
        Present: Verb ends in -a
        Past: Verb ends in -i
        Future: Verb ends in -o

4. Gender System
    Feminine Plural: -às
    Masculine Plural: -a
    Neutral Plural: -es

5. Other Systems

    No: Nào (masculine)
    Not: Na (plural form starting at the first letter that is the plural vowel, e.g. à for the -a masculine plural)
    Don't: Same as Not, You can optionally use do + na, however it is not recommended.

6. Exaggeration System

You can exaggerate or emphasise something with uppercase-only words. example:

YÒ TÈCERO!!

7. Compound System

Like german, just take any two words and add them together with no spaces. Go crazy with it!

examples:
óntò - on to
ites - it is
